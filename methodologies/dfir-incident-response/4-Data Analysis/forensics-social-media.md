## Major social media crimes, including those that involve the use of AI
1. **Phishing and Social Engineering**: Cybercriminals use AI to create highly convincing phishing emails and messages. AI can tailor these messages to specific individuals, making them more effective[1](https://www.fbi.gov/contact-us/field-offices/sanfrancisco/news/fbi-warns-of-increasing-threat-of-cyber-criminals-utilizing-artificial-intelligence).
2. **Deepfakes**: AI-generated deepfake videos and audio can be used to impersonate individuals, spread misinformation, or commit fraud. These can be highly realistic and difficult to detect[2](https://www.zdnet.com/article/evil-ai-these-are-the-20-most-dangerous-crimes-that-artificial-intelligence-will-create/).
3. **Cyberbullying and Harassment**: AI can be used to automate and amplify cyberbullying and harassment campaigns, making them more pervasive and harder to control[3](https://www.asisonline.org/security-management-magazine/articles/2024/06/deepfakes/generative-ai-crimes/).
4. **Identity Theft and Fraud**: AI tools can scrape social media for personal information, which can then be used to steal identities or commit fraud[4](https://blog.checkpoint.com/security/2025-cyber-security-predictions-the-rise-of-ai-driven-attacks-quantum-threats-and-social-media-exploitation/).
5. **Disinformation Campaigns**: AI can generate and spread fake news and disinformation on social media, influencing public opinion and causing social unrest[2](https://www.zdnet.com/article/evil-ai-these-are-the-20-most-dangerous-crimes-that-artificial-intelligence-will-create/).
6. **Voice and Video Cloning**: AI can clone voices and create fake videos to deceive people into giving away sensitive information or authorizing fraudulent transactions[1](https://www.fbi.gov/contact-us/field-offices/sanfrancisco/news/fbi-warns-of-increasing-threat-of-cyber-criminals-utilizing-artificial-intelligence).

These crimes highlight the growing need for robust security measures and awareness to protect against AI-enhanced threats on social media.

## Major challenges faced in social media forensics
1. **Data Volume and Variety**: Social media platforms generate vast amounts of data in various formats (text, images, videos, etc.), making it difficult to collect, process, and analyze all relevant information.
2. **Data Privacy and Legal Issues**: Accessing and using social media data often involves navigating complex privacy laws and regulations, which can vary by jurisdiction and platform.
3. **Encryption and Anonymity**: Many social media communications are encrypted, and users can remain anonymous or use pseudonyms, complicating the identification and tracking of individuals.
4. **Rapidly Changing Technology**: Social media platforms frequently update their features and security measures, requiring forensic experts to continuously adapt their tools and techniques.
5. **Data Authenticity and Integrity**: Ensuring that the data collected is authentic and has not been tampered with is crucial for forensic investigations. This includes dealing with deepfakes and other AI-generated content.
6. **Cross-Platform Investigations**: Criminal activities often span multiple social media platforms, requiring coordination and data integration from various sources.
7. **Resource Constraints**: Forensic investigations can be resource-intensive, requiring specialized tools, expertise, and significant time to analyze large datasets.
8. **Chain of Custody**: Maintaining a clear and documented chain of custody for digital evidence is essential to ensure its admissibility in court.

These challenges highlight the complexity of social media forensics and the need for advanced tools and expertise to effectively address them.

## Methods for collecting data from social media platforms in a forensics investigation
1. **Manual Collection**: Visiting social media account pages manually to capture data by taking screenshots or printing pages[1](https://legal.thomsonreuters.com/blog/social-media-forensics-in-law-enforcement-investigations/).
2. **Downloading Content**: Downloading copies of photos, videos, and other files directly from social media platforms[1](https://legal.thomsonreuters.com/blog/social-media-forensics-in-law-enforcement-investigations/).
3. **Metadata Analysis**: Reviewing metadata associated with posts, images, and videos to gather additional information such as timestamps and geolocation data[2](https://link.springer.com/chapter/10.1007/978-3-030-23547-5_14).
4. **Using Open Source Tools**: Utilizing open source or specialized software programs to collect and preserve social media data[1](https://legal.thomsonreuters.com/blog/social-media-forensics-in-law-enforcement-investigations/).
5. **Legal Requests**: Subpoenaing or obtaining court orders to compel social media companies to provide additional information and documents[1](https://legal.thomsonreuters.com/blog/social-media-forensics-in-law-enforcement-investigations/).
6. **Network Analysis**: Analyzing interactions and connections between users to identify relationships and patterns[3](https://www.forensicsinsider.com/digital-forensics/social-media-forensics/).
7. **Keyword Searches**: Conducting keyword searches to find relevant posts, comments, and messages[3](https://www.forensicsinsider.com/digital-forensics/social-media-forensics/).
8. **User Profiling**: Creating profiles of users based on their social media activity to understand their behavior and connections[3](https://www.forensicsinsider.com/digital-forensics/social-media-forensics/).

These methods help ensure that the data collected is comprehensive and can be used effectively in forensic investigations.
