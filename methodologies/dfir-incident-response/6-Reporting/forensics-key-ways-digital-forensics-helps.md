## Key Ways Digital Evidence Helps
Here are some key ways digital evidence helps during a security incident:

1. Identifying the nature and scope of the incident: By analyzing digital evidence such as log files, network traffic captures, and system artifacts, investigators can determine the type of attack, the entry point, and the extent of the compromise.[1][3] This information is essential for containing the incident and preventing further damage.

2. Reconstructing the attack timeline: Digital forensics techniques allow investigators to reconstruct the sequence of events leading up to and during the security incident.[2] This timeline helps understand the attacker's tactics, techniques, and procedures (TTPs), which can aid in attribution and future defense.

3. Gathering evidence for legal proceedings: Digital evidence collected through proper forensic methods can be admissible in court.[1][3] This evidence can be used to prosecute the attackers or support legal actions against them.

4. Identifying the root cause: By analyzing digital evidence, investigators can pinpoint the vulnerabilities or misconfigurations that were exploited by the attackers.[1][4] This information is crucial for remediation and preventing similar incidents in the future.

5. Assisting incident response and recovery: Digital forensics aids in devising strategies to contain the incident, remove malware, and recover from the attack.[2] It also helps in understanding the impact of the breach on the organization and its customers, enabling appropriate response measures.

6. Enabling threat intelligence: Digital evidence can reveal patterns of behavior, tools, and techniques used by specific threat actors.[2] This intelligence can be shared with the cybersecurity community to improve defenses against similar attacks.

In summary, digital evidence is invaluable in understanding the full scope of a security incident, attributing the attack, gathering legal evidence, identifying root causes, guiding incident response and recovery efforts, and contributing to threat intelligence.[1][2][3][4]

## Details that should be written on the Evidence Bag
When packaging evidence, the following details should be written on the evidence bag:

- Police case number or identification number [1][2]
- Date and time the evidence was collected [1][2]
- Description of the item (e.g. type of material, make, model, brand) [1][2]
- Location where the evidence was collected [2]
- Name and badge number of the officer(s) who collected the evidence [1][2]
- Any relevant serial numbers or other identifying information [2]
- Where the evidence is being sent for analysis [2]
- Appropriate labels like "BIOHAZARD" or "CONTAINS SHARP" if applicable [1][4]

It's crucial to clearly label the evidence bag with these details to establish the chain of custody, prevent contamination or mix-ups, and ensure proper handling and analysis of the evidence.[1][2][4] The labeling should be done on the outside of the bag, and care should be taken not to write on the packaging after the evidence is inside.[1]
