# Chain of Custody

## Definition
Chain of custody is a chronological, written roadmap that tracks every step an item of evidence takes from the exact moment it is collected at a crime scene until it is presented in a court of law. It is a continuous log that documents who found the evidence, who picked it up, who analyzed it, where it was stored, and when it changed hands.

## Why It Matters
In the real world, digital evidence can easily be changed, swapped, or altered. If an investigator cannot prove exactly who had possession of a hard drive or a phone at every single second, a defense lawyer can claim that someone tampered with the data. If there is even a short gap where the evidence is unaccounted for, the judge may throw the evidence out of the case completely. A perfect chain of custody guarantees that the data remains trusted and legally admissible.

## CHFI Exam Domain Mapping

## Scenario Example
A digital forensic investigator collects a USB thumb drive from a suspect's office desk at 10:00 AM. The investigator immediately places the drive inside an anti-static bag and fills out the first line of a chain of custody form, signing their name and listing the exact date, time, and location.

Later that afternoon, the investigator takes the bag to the police forensics lab and hands it to a lab technician. Before letting go of the bag, both the investigator and the technician sign the form, noting the exact minute the transfer happened. When the technician finishes creating a forensic image of the drive, they lock the original USB drive inside a secure evidence locker and log that action on the form. Because every single movement was recorded, the chain remains unbroken.

## Skills and Competencies Learned
- **Evidence Documentation**: The ability to accurately fill out, maintain, and audit an official forensic log sheet.
- **Secure Evidence Handling**: Knowing the proper physical security steps, such as using tamper-evident bags and secure storage lockers, to protect physical devices.
- **Process Accountability**: Developing the professional discipline to record every interaction with digital evidence to withstand cross-examination in court.
