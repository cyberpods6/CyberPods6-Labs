## Roles of the first responder
When responding to a crime scene, the key responsibilities of first responders include:
1. Securing the scene and controlling access:
```Establishing a perimeter with the crime scene tape and restricting entry to authorized personnel only [1][2][4]. Preventing contamination or alteration of evidence [1][2][4] ```
2. Ensuring safety: Scanning for potential threats or hazards that could endanger responders or others [1][4].
```Requesting backup or specialized units (bomb squad, SWAT, etc.) if needed [1] ```
3. Rendering emergency aid: Providing immediate medical attention to any injured victims [1][2][4].
```Capturing dying declarations from critically injured victims [1] ```
4. Preserving and protecting evidence: Avoid contamination by limiting movement and contact within the scene [1][2][4].
```Documenting the initial scene conditions through notes, photos/videos [1][4].  Identifying potential evidence but not collecting or moving items [2][4] ```
5. Obtaining information: Identifying and separating witnesses for subsequent interviews [1][2].
```Broadcasting lookout information or suspect descriptions to other units [1]. Determining entry/exit routes used by suspects or victims [1]```
6. Maintaining control until investigators arrive: Establishing a crime scene log to document all entries/exits [1].
```Briefing investigators and relinquishing the scene after proper transfer [1][2].```

The overarching duty is to quickly stabilize and preserve the integrity of the crime scene until forensic investigators can properly process and collect evidence.[1][2][4] Meticulous scene management by first responders is crucial for successful investigations and prosecutions.

## Some of the first steps of a First Responder on the Scene
1. Assess the situation quickly to determine the scope and severity of the incident[1][2].
```This includes identifying affected systems, potential data breaches, and immediate threats.```
2. Isolate affected systems by disconnecting them from the network to prevent further spread of the threat[1][3].
```This containment step is crucial to limit damage.```
3. Preserve evidence by creating forensic images of affected systems' memory and storage devices[3].
```This captures the current state for later analysis without altering the original evidence.```
4. Document everything, including symptoms, unusual behavior, and actions taken so far[3].
```Thorough documentation is essential for the investigation and potential legal proceedings.```
5. Identify and interview key personnel who discovered the incident or have relevant information[2].
```This can provide valuable context and details about the incident.```
6. Review logs and alerts from security tools like SIEM, antivirus, and intrusion detection systems to gather more information about the incident[4].

7. Establish a secure communication channel for the incident response team to share information and coordinate efforts[1].

8. Begin initial triage and analysis to determine the root cause, attack vector, and potential impact of the incident[2][4].

9. Notify appropriate stakeholders, including management, legal counsel, and potentially affected parties, based on the incident response plan and regulatory requirements[2][3].

10. Implement immediate containment measures to prevent further damage or data loss, such as changing passwords or applying emergency patches[1][3].

Remember, the goal at this stage is to quickly assess the situation, contain the threat, preserve evidence, and set the stage for a thorough investigation and recovery process.
