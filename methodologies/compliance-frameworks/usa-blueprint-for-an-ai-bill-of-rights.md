## White House Office of Science and Technology Policy's "Blueprint for an AI Bill of Rights"
("Blueprint for an AI Bill of Rights | OSTP | The White House," 2023)
The article discusses the White House Office of Science and Technology Policy's "Blueprint for an AI Bill of Rights," which outlines five key principles to guide the development and use of artificial intelligence and automated systems in a way that protects American citizens' rights and values. This framework aims to address the challenges posed by technology and automated systems that may threaten civil rights, privacy, and democratic values.

Here's a bullet point list of the main topics with brief descriptions:

- Safe and Effective Systems: Automated systems should be developed with input from diverse stakeholders, undergo thorough testing, and be continuously monitored to ensure safety and effectiveness.

- Algorithmic Discrimination Protections: AI systems should be designed and used equitably, with proactive measures taken to prevent discrimination based on protected characteristics.

- Data Privacy: Individuals should be protected from abusive data practices through built-in safeguards and have control over how their data is collected, used, and shared.

- Notice and Explanation: People should be informed when an automated system is being used and understand how it affects outcomes that impact them.

- Human Alternatives, Consideration, and Fallback: Users should have the option to opt out of automated systems where appropriate and have access to human intervention for problem-solving and appeals.

The Blueprint for an AI Bill of Rights is intended to apply to automated systems that have the potential to significantly impact Americans' rights, opportunities, or access to critical resources or services. It aims to provide a comprehensive framework for protecting civil liberties and promoting responsible AI development and deployment.
