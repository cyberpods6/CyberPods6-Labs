# 20241228-eu-mandates-usb-c-standard
## EU common charger rules: Power all your devices with a single charger
The EU has implemented a universal charger for electronic devices to reduce waste. All new devices sold in the EU must now support USB-C charging.

The European Union has introduced new regulations requiring all mobile phones and portable electronic devices sold in the EU to support USB-C charging, effective December 28, 2024. This 

initiative aims to reduce electronic waste, enhance consumer convenience, and save money by eliminating the need for multiple chargers. The rules will extend to laptops starting April 28, 2026.
