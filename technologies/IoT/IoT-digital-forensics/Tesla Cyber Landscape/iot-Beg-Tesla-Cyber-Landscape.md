# Forensic Analysis of IoT Devices and Telematics for Automobiles
## Definition

Forensic analysis of IoT (Internet of Things) devices and automobile telematics involves collecting, preserving, and analyzing digital evidence from smart objects and connected vehicle systems. IoT forensics focuses on extracting data from interconnected sensors, smart home appliances, and wearable tech. Automobile telematics forensics focuses on data retrieved from vehicle system modules, infotainment centers, and onboard GPS navigation units that track a vehicle's status, location, and behavior.

## Why It Matters

In the real world, IoT devices and vehicles are constantly recording data that can establish an indisputable timeline of events. For a digital forensic investigator, telematics can reveal critical trip history, speed, acceleration patterns, door openings, and connected mobile device logs during an accident or criminal event. Similarly, IoT forensics can provide ambient data (such as smart lock timestamps or security camera activity logs) that can corroborate or disprove an alibi.

## Scenario Example
An investigator is assigned to look into a hit and run accident. The suspect claims their vehicle was parked at home during the collision. The investigator forensically acquires the vehicle's telematics system. The extracted navigation logs reveal that the car was active at the exact coordinates of the crash scene during the time of the event. Furthermore, Bluetooth logs show the suspect's phone was paired with the car's entertainment system at that exact moment, completely disproving the alibi.

## Skills and Competencies Learned
* Understanding the lifecycle of data transmission within embedded hardware and smart architectures.
* Methods for isolating connected devices from cellular or wireless networks to prevent remote data wiping or alteration (spoliation).

   * **S0242:** Skill to analyze loop traces, sensor data logs, and telematics files to reconstruct a historical timeline of device activity.
