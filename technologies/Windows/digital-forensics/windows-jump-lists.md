# windows-jump-lists
**Windows Jump Lists** are a feature introduced in Windows 7 and continued in later versions, including Windows 10 and Windows 11. They provide quick access to recently or frequently used files, folders, and tasks associated with specific applications. Here’s a detailed overview:

## What Are Jump Lists?
Jump Lists are context-specific menus that appear when you right-click on an application icon pinned to the taskbar or in the Start Menu. They display a list of items related to that application, such as:

- **Recently opened files**: Documents, images, or other files you’ve recently accessed using the application.
- **Frequently used files**: Files you open often with the application.
- **Pinned items**: Files or tasks you’ve manually pinned to the Jump List for quick access.
- **Application-specific tasks**: Actions or shortcuts specific to the application, like opening a new window, composing a new email, or accessing settings.

## How to Use Jump Lists
1. **Accessing Jump Lists**:
   - **Taskbar**: Right-click on an application icon pinned to the taskbar.
   - **Start Menu**: Right-click on an application in the Start Menu.

2. **Pinning Items**:
   - To pin an item to a Jump List, right-click on the item within the Jump List and select "Pin to this list".
   - Pinned items remain in the Jump List even if they are not recently or frequently used.

3. **Unpinning Items**:
   - To unpin an item, right-click on the pinned item and select "Unpin from this list".

4. **Customizing Jump Lists**:
   - You can control the number of items shown in Jump Lists through the Settings app. Go to **Settings > Personalization > Start** and adjust the "Show recently opened items in Jump Lists on Start or the taskbar" option.

## Benefits of Jump Lists
- **Efficiency**: Quickly access your most-used files and tasks without navigating through folders.
- **Customization**: Pin important files and tasks for easy access.
- **Productivity**: Streamline your workflow by reducing the time spent searching for files.

## Example
For instance, if you frequently use Microsoft Word, the Jump List for Word might include:
- Recently opened documents.
- Frequently used templates.
- Pinned documents that you need quick access to.
- Tasks like opening a new document or accessing Word settings.

Jump Lists are a handy feature for improving productivity and efficiency by providing quick access to the items you use most often with your applications.
